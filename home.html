Places to Visit in Dehradun

  • Top Attractions and Hidden Gems in Dehradun

    Dehradun, the gateway to Uttarakhand, offers a unique mix of cultural heritage and natural beauty, making it one of the most sought-after destinations in northern India. Among the must-visit places in Dehradun is the historic Tapkeshwar Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, where water continuously drips over the Shiva Lingam. Another iconic landmark is the Mindrolling Monastery, a center of Tibetan Buddhism known for its serene environment and impressive architecture. The local markets of Dehradun, like Paltan Bazaar, are great places to explore traditional crafts, textiles, and handmade goods that reflect the region's cultural vibrancy. The city also hosts colorful festivals, including Basant Panchami and Ganga Dussehra, which further highlight the rich cultural fabric of the region.

  • Nature's Delight in Dehradun

    Dehradun is renowned for its lush green surroundings and stunning landscapes, making it a paradise for nature lovers. Among the top places to visit in Dehradun is Sahastradhara, famous for its cascading waterfalls and healing sulfur springs. For wildlife enthusiasts, Rajaji National Park offers a thrilling adventure on the outskirts of the city, where you might spot elephants, tigers, and other wildlife. If you're looking for a peaceful retreat, visit Malsi Deer Park for a relaxing day among nature, or head to Lachhiwala Nature Park to enjoy a serene boat ride and a family picnic amidst beautiful greenery.


Unveil the Charm of Dehradun: Your Doorway to the Himalayas

Dehradun, the capital of Uttarakhand, India, is nestled in the scenic foothills of the Himalayas. Renowned for its picturesque landscapes, pleasant climate, and rich cultural heritage, the city offers a mix of natural beauty and urban development, making it a desired destination for tourists and residents alike.

Home to prestigious institutions like the Indian Military Academy (IMA) and the Forest Research Institute (FRI), Dehradun also stands out as an educational hub. These institutions underscore the city's academic prestige and its role as a center for learning and research.

Best Places to Visit in Dehradun

Dehradun is more than just a gateway to popular destinations like Mussoorie , Rishikesh , and Haridwar . The city itself boasts remarkable landmarks, including Robber's Cave (Gucchupani), known for its mysterious caves, and Sahastradhara, famous for its healing sulfur springs. Don’t miss the iconic Clock Tower and serene spots like Tapkeshwar Temple and Malsi Deer Park, perfect for nature enthusiasts.

Cultural and Culinary Highlights of Dehradun

Dive into Dehradun’s rich food culture with local Garhwali and Kumaoni flavors, offering a culinary adventure for those seeking regional delicacies. From bustling markets to serene spots, Dehradun captures the essence of tranquility and adventure.

The city's vibrant heritage blends ancient temples, modern educational institutions, and lively markets. Whether enjoying local street food or shopping for traditional crafts, visitors can immerse themselves in Dehradun's unique atmosphere.

How to Reach Dehradun

Dehradun is well-connected by road, rail, and air, making it easily accessible from major cities in India:

  • By Air: The nearest airport is Jolly Grant Airport, about 25 km from the city center, with regular flights from Delhi, Mumbai, and other major cities.
  • By Train: Dehradun Railway Station connects well to cities like Delhi, Kolkata, and Mumbai. Popular trains include the Shatabdi Express and Nanda Devi Express.
  • By Road: Dehradun is connected by national highways, with frequent bus services from Delhi, Haridwar, Rishikesh, and other nearby towns.

When to Visit Dehradun

The best time to visit Dehradun is between March and June and September and November. These months offer pleasant weather, ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities. While the monsoon season (July to August) brings lush greenery, it also comes with heavy rains. Winters (December to February) can be quite cold, especially in the evenings.

Best Places Near Dehradun

Dehradun's strategic location makes it an excellent base for exploring nearby attractions:

  • Mussoorie: Known as the "Queen of Hills," just 35 km away, offering breathtaking views, waterfalls, and colonial-era architecture.
  • Rishikesh: About 45 km from Dehradun, Rishikesh is a spiritual hub with ashrams, yoga centers, and adventure sports like river rafting.
  • Haridwar: A holy city 55 km away, famous for its Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri and numerous temples.
  • Dhanaulti: A serene hill station 60 km from Dehradun, known for its peaceful environment, pine forests, and snow-capped peaks.
  • Chakrata: Located 90 km away, Chakrata offers beautiful trekking trails, caves, and stunning landscapes, ideal for adventure enthusiasts.

Explore the Serene Forest Trails

Dehradun offers numerous beautiful and tranquil forest trails. Some of the top places to explore include:

  • Rajaji National Park: Ideal for wildlife lovers, offering sightings of elephants, tigers, and leopards.
  • Malsi Deer Park: A family-friendly spot perfect for picnics and enjoying nature.
  • Forest Research Institute (FRI): Known for its academic prestige and expansive grounds, perfect for nature walks.

Hidden Waterfalls in Dehradun

  • Bhatta Falls: A less crowded and peaceful spot, perfect for picnicking and relaxing.
  • Shikhar Falls: A hidden gem ideal for rock climbing, nature photography, and exploration.

Dehradun’s vibrant culture shines through its local festivals:

  • Magh Mela: A grand event filled with fairs, music, and dance.
  • Jhanda Mela: A major fair celebrated at Guru Ram Rai Darbar, attracting devotees from all over India.

Dehradun’s markets offer a wide variety of traditional crafts and souvenirs:

  • Wooden Handicrafts: Beautifully carved wooden items, perfect for home décor and gifting.
  • Pashmina Shawls: Famous for their fine quality, these shawls are popular among tourists.

Dehradun for Wellness and Spiritual Retreats

  • Ananda in the Himalayas: A luxury wellness retreat offering yoga, meditation, and Ayurvedic therapies.
  • Vishranti Nature Resort: A nature and wellness resort offering yoga and holistic health treatments.

Dehradun also offers peaceful spiritual retreats:

  • Mindrolling Monastery: One of the largest Buddhist centers in India, offering a serene atmosphere for meditation and reflection.
  • Tapkeshwar Temple: A temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located inside a natural cave, perfect for pilgrims and nature lovers alike.

Adventure Beyond the City: Day Trips from Dehradun

If you're staying in Dehradun, consider these exciting day trips to nearby attractions:

  • Lachhiwala Nature Park: Located 22 km from the city, this park offers natural pools, trekking trails, and picnic spots.
  • Tibetan Buddhist Temple: A peaceful temple complex with a stupa and prayer hall featuring stunning murals.

    • Kempty Falls: A popular waterfall located 13 km from Mussoorie.
    • Gun Hill: The second-highest point in Mussoorie, offering panoramic views of the Himalayas.

Best Trekking Places Near Dehradun

Discover the best trekking destinations near Dehradun that offer stunning views, rich biodiversity, and unforgettable experiences. Whether you're a seasoned trekker or a beginner, these treks provide something for everyone looking to explore the majestic Himalayas.



  • Har Ki Dun Trek

    The Har Ki Dun Trek takes you to the stunning 'Valley of Gods' within the Govind Pashu Vihar Wildlife Sanctuary. This trek is steeped in ancient culture and natural beauty, featuring breathtaking views of snow-capped peaks, pristine rivers, and lush valleys. With its rich flora and fauna, Har Ki Dun is perfect for nature enthusiasts looking to explore the lesser-known treasures of the Himalayas. This moderate-level trek is a journey through time and nature's marvels.

    • Ancient cultural trail
    • Rivers and meadows
    • Moderate difficulty level
    • Best from April to June & September to November

    Read More »

  • Chandrashila Trek

    The Chandrashila Trek is a thrilling and moderately challenging adventure that takes you through the scenic landscapes of Chopta, known as the 'Mini Switzerland of India.' This trek culminates at the summit of Chandrashila, offering panoramic views of Himalayan peaks like Nanda Devi, Trishul, and Chaukhamba. Along the way, trekkers pass through Tungnath, the highest Shiva temple in the world, adding a spiritual essence to the journey. Ideal for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts, Chandrashila Trek promises breathtaking vistas and a memorable experience in the Garhwal Himalayas.

    • Himalayan peak views (Nanda Devi, Trishul)
    • Best from April to June & September to November
    • Visit to Tungnath Temple
    • Great for bird watching and wildlife spotting

    Read More »

  • Dayara Bugyal Trek

    The Dayara Bugyal Trek is known for its sprawling meadows that stretch far into the horizon. Situated at an altitude of 3,408 meters, Dayara Bugyal is a trek through lush pastures, dense oak forests, and panoramic views of the Himalayan peaks. Suitable for beginners and families, this trek offers a serene and picturesque setting, especially in spring and summer when the meadows are in full bloom.

    • Panoramic views of Himalayan peaks
    • Best from May to June & September to November
    • Ideal for beginners and families
    • Duration: 4-5 days, approx. 20-25 km
